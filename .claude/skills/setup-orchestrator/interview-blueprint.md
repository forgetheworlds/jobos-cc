# Interview Blueprint — Deep Initialization Guide

## Philosophy
This is not a form fill. It is a structured deep interview that turns a vague candidate into a strong data foundation. Every section should probe, not just record.

## Interview Flow

### Section A: Identity & Contact
*Priority: HIGH — data is used in every portal fill*

Required fields:
- Full legal name (as on official documents)
- Preferred display name (for cover letters)
- Phone (international format)
- Email (professional)
- LinkedIn URL (full, not shortened)
- Current location (city, province/state, country)
- Relocation willingness: yes / open / no
- Remote/hybrid/on-site preference: ranked
- Work authorization status (citizen, PR, visa type)
- Citizenship if relevant to postings

Probing questions:
- "Are you willing to relocate for the right role, or only for certain locations?"
- "Do you have any geographic constraints (family, visa, etc.)?"

### Section B: Professional Narrative
*Priority: HIGH — shapes every piece of tailored content*

Must capture:
1. One-sentence professional identity
2. Three strongest career themes
3. Which past roles felt most aligned
4. What work to do more of
5. What work to avoid
6. Three things a hiring manager should remember

Probing questions:
- "If someone read your resume and described you in one sentence, what should they say?"
- "What do you bring that most candidates in your space don't?"
- "What pattern connects your best career moves?"

### Section C: Full Experience Extraction
*Priority: CRITICAL — this is the source of truth for all materials*

For each major role, extract:
- Exact title
- Employer name (as it appears officially)
- Dates (month/year to month/year or "present")
- Location
- Reporting structure if known
- Main responsibilities (3-5 core duties)
- Leadership scope (who/what managed: staff, faculty, students, budgets)
- Specific metrics (numbers served, programs managed, budgets overseen)
- Systems/tools used (LMS, SIS, specific software)
- Populations served (student types, faculty groups, community partners)
- Achievements with measurable outcomes
- Collaboration examples (cross-departmental, multi-stakeholder)
- Policy/process examples (created, improved, implemented)
- Strongest stories (memorable successes)

DO NOT stop at resume bullets. Probe deeper:
- "What did you improve in this role?"
- "How large was the program you managed?"
- "Who did you coordinate with regularly?"
- "What kinds of students did you support?"
- "Did you manage schedules, faculty, budgets, or resources?"
- "Did you build new processes or improve existing ones?"
- "Any experience with accreditation, quality assurance, or evaluation?"
- "Did you lead orientation, transition, retention, or first-year initiatives?"

### Section D: Education & Credentials
- All degrees with field, institution, year
- Certifications (current and expired)
- Ongoing education (workshops, conferences, MOOCs)
- Academic specializations
- Languages with proficiency levels

### Section E: Skills & Strengths Inventory
Rate each skill (strong / competent / developing / no experience) AND ask for evidence:

1. Program management
2. Student advising
3. Faculty coordination
4. Curriculum design
5. Academic operations
6. Workshop facilitation
7. Training and mentoring
8. Policy implementation
9. Data-informed program evaluation
10. Cross-cultural communication
11. Stakeholder engagement
12. Academic writing
13. Conflict handling
14. Committee support
15. Governance support
16. Institutional systems familiarity
17. Technology comfort
18. Public speaking

For each "strong" rating, ask: "Give me a specific example."

### Section F: Target Role Prioritization
Present all role family lanes. For each, ask:
- "Is this a primary target, secondary, fallback, or exclude?"
- "Which specific titles in this cluster appeal most?"
- "Are any titles in this cluster misleading?"

Also capture:
- Salary bands (minimum, preferred)
- Institution types (R1, community college, liberal arts, etc.)
- Sector preferences (public, private, non-profit)

### Section G: Geography & Work Arrangements
- Preferred cities (ranked)
- Maximum commute
- Remote openness
- Hybrid minimum days
- Relocation assistance needed?
- Preferred provinces/states/countries
- Contract role tolerance

### Section H: Writing Style & Tone
- Formal vs. warm institutional tone?
- Concise vs. detailed letters?
- Phrases to absolutely avoid
- Values to foreground
- Examples of writing the candidate likes
- Examples of writing the candidate dislikes
- Cover letter style: traditional / modern / mission-driven / pragmatic

### Section I: Behavioral & Interview Assets
Gather STAR stories for:
1. Leadership
2. Conflict resolution
3. Student support
4. Faculty collaboration
5. Program improvement
6. Change management
7. Inclusive practice
8. Problem solving
9. Policy application
10. Stakeholder coordination
11. Mentoring

Template: "Tell me about a time when [situation]. What did you do, and what happened?"

### Section J: Screening Question Library
Build draft seeds for:
1. "Why are you interested in this role?"
2. "Describe your experience supporting students."
3. "Describe your experience coordinating programs."
4. "Tell us about your work with faculty."
5. "Describe your approach to inclusion and equity."
6. "Tell us about a time you improved a program."
7. "Tell us about your experience in academic advising."
8. "Describe your curriculum development experience."
9. "Tell us about experience with policy or quality assurance."

### Section K: Search Strategy Configuration
- Target titles (from clusters, ranked)
- Synonyms for each title
- Must-have keywords
- Optional keywords
- Excluded keywords
- Preferred institution types
- Preferred portals
- Search frequency
- Aggressiveness for borderline roles

### Section L: Constraints & Red Lines
- Jobs to never apply to automatically
- Titles to never target
- Salary floors
- Contract tolerance
- Travel tolerance
- Unacceptable work arrangements
- Institutions to avoid
- May the system ever submit without explicit approval?

## End-of-Setup Self-Check
After completing all sections, the system must ask itself:
- What is still unclear?
- What data gaps remain?
- What evidence is weak or unverified?
- Which role families need more examples?
- Which answers need human confirmation?
- Which profile areas should be enriched later?

Generate `open_questions.md` with all identified gaps.

## Improvement Mandate
The system should proactively suggest:
- Title expansions ("You should also target X")
- Stronger positioning ("This experience should be reframed")
- Missing metrics ("This role needs specific numbers")
- Underused angles ("Your first-year leadership is underrepresented")
- Better examples ("You need a stronger faculty development story")

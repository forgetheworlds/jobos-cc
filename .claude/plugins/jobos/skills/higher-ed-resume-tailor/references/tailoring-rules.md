# Resume Tailoring Rules — Higher Education

## Core Philosophy

Tailoring means **reorganizing truth** — changing ordering and emphasis, never content. You reorder bullet points, expand relevant ones slightly, and compress less relevant ones. You align terminology where it honestly applies. You never add, inflate, or invent.

## What Tailoring IS

- Reordering experience sections to foreground the most relevant roles
- Expanding bullet points that directly address the job's top requirements
- Compressing or condensing less relevant experience
- Matching terminology: if the job says "student retention" and the candidate has "first-year student success", use both terms where they honestly overlap
- Adjusting the professional summary to match the lane
- Reordering the skills section by relevance to this specific role

## What Tailoring is NOT

- Adding skills the candidate does not have
- Inflating titles or responsibilities
- Altering dates, institutions, or outcomes
- Fabricating achievements or metrics
- Removing relevant experience just to shorten the resume
- Claiming proficiency in tools or systems not in resume_facts.yml

## Professional Summary Templates

The professional summary (2-3 sentences) should be tailored to the lane:

- **academic_admin**: "Experienced higher education leader with [X] years directing academic programs, coordinating faculty across departments, and improving student outcomes at scale. Led first-year program serving 2,000+ students annually with demonstrated improvement in completion rates."
- **student_success**: "Student-centered higher education professional with [X] years advancing student success through strategic advising, retention programming, and inclusive student engagement across diverse institutional settings."
- **curriculum_faculty_dev**: "Curriculum and faculty development specialist with [X] years designing program assessments, facilitating faculty training, and improving instructional quality in higher education settings."

Adapt these templates to reflect the specific job's language — but keep claims grounded in real experience.

## Bullet Point Guidelines

- Start with strong action verbs: Directed, Led, Designed, Coordinated, Implemented, Facilitated, Mentored, Evaluated
- Include quantified outcomes where available from resume_facts: "2,000+ students annually", "15% improvement", "reduced attrition"
- Keep each bullet to 1-2 lines
- For the MOST relevant experience section: 5-7 bullets with specific outcomes
- For secondary experience: 3-4 bullets, more condensed
- For least relevant: 2-3 bullets, high-level summary

## Fact-Check Before Writing

Before finalizing the resume, verify every bullet point:
- [ ] Does this experience exist in resume_facts.yml?
- [ ] Are the dates, titles, and institutions correct?
- [ ] Are the outcomes stated actually supported by the facts?
- [ ] Is any terminology alignment honest (the candidate actually did this thing)?

# Behavioral Profile — Dr. Mohamed Sambul
# Professional strengths, preferences, and working style.
# Used by the Fit Analyst and Cover Letter Crafter for culture-fit assessment.

## Professional Strengths

1. **Student-Centered Leadership**: Natural orientation toward student success and wellbeing,
   with deep understanding of the challenges facing first-year and underserved students.
   *Evidence: Directed first-year program serving 2,000+ students; reduced attrition through coordinated advising.*

2. **Cross-Cultural Communication**: Over 20 years of experience working across cultural
   contexts, with genuine comfort and skill in multicultural environments.
   *Evidence: Served as Community Scholar at York University's Centre for Refugee Studies; fluent in 4 languages.*

3. **Program Development**: Ability to design, implement, and assess academic programs
   from concept to operation — not just incremental improvement, but building new
   structures from the ground up.
   *Evidence: Led program evaluation resulting in estimated 15% improvement in course completion rates.*

4. **Collaborative Faculty Relations**: Track record of working effectively with faculty
   across departments, respecting academic freedom while maintaining program coherence.
   *Evidence: Designed faculty training sessions that improved teaching quality and consistency across courses.*

5. **Community Partnerships**: Experience building and maintaining productive relationships
   between academic institutions and community organizations.
   *Evidence: Supervised practicum placements at Pathways to Education–York University partnership (2010-2015).*

6. **Mentoring & Development**: Invests in student growth through structured mentoring
   and reflective practice facilitation.
   *Evidence: Mentored students in academic development, research projects, and career planning; facilitated reflective practice sessions improving practicum students' instructional techniques.*

## Work Environment Preferences

- **Thrives in**: Mission-driven organizations, collaborative teams, student-facing roles,
  environments that value diversity and inclusion, higher education institutions
- **Energized by**: Seeing student success outcomes improve, developing new programs,
  mentoring colleagues, building partnerships, faculty development
- **Drained by**: Purely administrative tasks without student impact, highly competitive
  internal politics, roles focused primarily on compliance without improvement, K-12
  teaching without leadership component, sales-oriented education roles
- **Best team role**: Coordinator, bridge-builder, mentor — someone who connects people
  and ideas across boundaries; servant leader who enables others' success

## Leadership Style

**Collaborative Servant Leadership**
- Leads by supporting faculty, student, and staff success
- Emphasizes shared governance and cross-departmental coordination
- Builds consensus while maintaining program quality
- Communicates rationale clearly and provides training/support resources
- Establishes ongoing feedback mechanisms to prevent conflicts

- **Results-oriented** — focuses on outcomes, not just activities
- **Comfortable making independent decisions** when necessary, especially within program domain

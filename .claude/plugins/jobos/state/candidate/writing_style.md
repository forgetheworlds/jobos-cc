# Writing Style Guide — Dr. Mohamed Sambul
# Spec §11.5-H: Defines the voice, tone, and language conventions for all generated materials.

## Tone

**Formal-warm institutional** — professional but approachable. Think: a respected dean
writing to a colleague, not a corporate recruiter writing to a stranger.

- Confident but not boastful
- Warm but not casual
- Specific but not verbose
- Mission-driven but not preachy
- Active voice throughout

## Register

- **Resume**: Concise, achievement-oriented, third-person implied (no "I")
- **Cover letter**: First person, conversational professional, forward-looking
- **Screening responses**: First person, direct, evidence-backed
- **All materials**: Match the formality of higher education without being stuffy

## Language Rules

### Must Do
- Use active verbs: "Coordinated faculty across 5 departments" not "Was responsible for faculty coordination"
- Be specific with numbers and outcomes when available
- Reference higher-education terminology correctly (e.g., "program review" not "project review")
- Use institution-appropriate language (match their terminology from the posting)
- Lead with impact, follow with method

### Must Not
- Use em dashes (—) or en dashes (–). Use commas, periods, or hyphens instead.
- Use corporate buzzwords: "leverage," "synergy," "ecosystem," "paradigm," "robust"
- Use filler enthusiasm: "I am passionate about," "I am excited to," "I believe I am a great fit"
- Use vague claims without evidence: "proven track record," "extensive experience," "strong communicator"
- Use apologetic hedging: "I think I could contribute," "I might be able to"
- Use LLM tells: "furthermore," "additionally," "moreover," "it is worth noting"
- Fabricate any claim, credential, skill, or experience

### Banned Phrases (will be auto-rejected by validator)
- "passionate about"
- "dedicated to"
- "committed to excellence"
- "leverage my skills"
- "hit the ground running"
- "synergy"
- "robust"
- "cutting-edge"
- "proven track record"
- "strong communicator"
- "team player"
- "fast learner"
- "self-starter"
- "detail-oriented"
- "deeply committed"
- "I am confident that"

## Higher-Ed-Specific Style

### Use Correct Terminology
- "Faculty" not "teachers" (in higher-ed context)
- "Academic programs" not "courses" (when referring to degree programs)
- "Student success" not "student performance"
- "Retention" not "keeping students"
- "Enrolled students" not "customers"
- "Assessment" not "testing" (for program evaluation)
- "Accreditation" not "certification" (for institutional quality)

### Mission Alignment Language
When referencing an institution's mission:
- Be genuine — don't just parrot their mission statement
- Connect specific aspects of the mission to specific experiences
- Show understanding of what the mission means in practice
- Avoid generic "I share your commitment to..." statements

## Cover Letter Structure (Higher-Ed Specific)

1. **Opening**: Connect specific experience to the role — not "I am writing to apply"
2. **Fit paragraph**: 2-3 most relevant achievements with evidence
3. **Mission paragraph**: Genuine connection to institution's mission, values, or specific programs
4. **Close**: Brief, confident — "I welcome the opportunity to discuss how my experience..."

Target length: 350-450 words (one page).

## Resume Conventions

- **Summary**: 3-4 sentences positioning candidate for the specific lane
- **Experience**: Reverse chronological, 3-5 bullets per role, most relevant bullets first
- **Bullet format**: Action verb + what + quantified impact (when available)
- **Education**: Degrees with field, institution, year
- **No skills matrix** — weave skills into experience bullets
- **Dr. prefix on name** — always include
